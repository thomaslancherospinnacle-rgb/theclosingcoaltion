Americans Often Don't Know What Life Insurance They Have

According to LIMRA's 2025 Life Insurance Workplace Benefits report, more than 1 in 3 workers are not fully aware they even have employer-provided life insurance. National surveys reveal millions of adults don't know if they have life insurance at all.

Knowledge Gap

41% of Americans say they are only somewhat or not at all knowledgeable about life insurance.

Most families are unknowingly under-protected.

Coverage Misconception

Among people who do have life insurance, 22–40% admit they don't have enough coverage.

Employer coverage is usually only 1–2× annual salary and ends when employment ends.

The Financial Burden on Working-Class Families

When income stops, bills don't. For regular working families, the loss of a spouse or parent means immediate financial crisis:

Immediate Losses

  • Income replacement lost
  • Mortgage or rent payments continue
  • Car loans and insurance due
  • Credit cards and personal debt
  • Childcare and education costs

Funeral & Final Expenses

Average funeral cost in the U.S.: $7,000–$10,000+

Without life insurance, families often rely on:

  • Credit cards
  • Fundraisers
  • Personal loans

Life insurance prevents families from being forced into debt at the worst possible moment.

Military & Service Families Face Unique Risks

Military families often assume government benefits alone are sufficient — but gaps are common.

Common Challenges

  • Frequent relocation
  • Deployment risks
  • Income changes during service transitions
  • Benefits that may not fully replace long-term civilian income

Even when service members have coverage, it may:

  • Not travel with them after service
  • Not be enough for mortgage + children + spouse
  • Require additional planning outside military benefits

Life insurance ensures long-term stability, not just short-term assistance.

Cost Is the #1 Myth Stopping Families

Corebridge Financial research shows that nearly half of Americans overestimate the cost of life insurance or aren't sure how much it costs.

The Reality

Healthy young adults often overestimate premiums by 7–12 times the actual cost.

Only about 25% of adults can correctly estimate the cost of a basic term policy.

Actual Cost

Many families can secure coverage for far less than expected, often comparable to:

  • A streaming subscription
  • A phone bill add-on
  • Weekly coffee purchases

Families delay protection — not because they don't care — but because they've been misinformed.
